# earth_eater
This project investigates the question, “Are we eating away our Earth to survive?” through a data-driven analysis aligned with Sustainable Development Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. Using publicly available datasets from Our World in Data, the presentation examines long-term global trends in population growth, per-capita caloric supply, total food demand (calculated by multiplying population by calorie intake), agricultural land expansion, forest share decline, and global temperature anomalies. The analysis follows a structured progression: first establishing the dramatic rise in human population after 1950, then demonstrating how increasing per-person consumption amplified total food demand, which in turn required expanded agricultural land and contributed to declining forest coverage. Finally, rising global temperature anomalies are presented as a broader environmental response to cumulative human pressures. The findings suggest that environmental strain is not driven solely by population size nor simply by overeating, but by how food is produced, distributed, and consumed at scale.
